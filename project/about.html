About Amazon

Amazon is one of the largest and most influential companies in the world. Founded by Jeff Bezos in 1994, it started as an online bookstore but quickly evolved into a global e-commerce and technology giant. Here’s an overview of Amazon and its main areas of focus

E-Commerce:

Amazon’s primary business is its online retail platform, which offers millions of products across various categories such as electronics, clothing, home goods, groceries, books, and more. Customers can buy items from both Amazon itself and third-party sellers who use Amazon's platform to reach a global audience

Amazon Web Services:

AWS is one of Amazon’s most profitable and fastest-growing segments. It provides cloud computing services such as data storage, computing power, and machine learning tools to businesses, governments, and organizations around the world. AWS is a dominant player in the cloud computing industry, alongside competitors like Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud

Amazon Logistics & Delivery

Amazon has built an extensive logistics network to handle its vast e-commerce operations, including fulfillment centers, warehouses, and its own delivery services like Amazon Prime Delivery, Amazon Flex, and Amazon Air. In recent years, Amazon has started to handle more of its deliveries independently, investing in drones, self-driving technology, and last-mile delivery solutions.
